Parents & Teachers

Gentle books and activities for curious young readers.

Lulu & Ellie books are designed to be warm, playful, encouraging, and emotionally safe.

The stories and activities use curiosity, humor, kindness, and problem-solving instead of fear, shame, or pressure.

The goal is simple: help children feel brave, capable, curious, and ready to try.

Designed for
Families, classrooms, libraries, and early readers.
Gentle by design

Books that invite children to keep going, not shut down.

Reading Confidence

Reading confidence

Lulu & Ellie books encourage children to enjoy stories, notice details, follow clues, retell what happened, and return to favorite worlds.

The tone keeps reading welcoming so children can practice with confidence and curiosity.

How to Use Storybooks

How to use storybooks

The storybook series work well as read-alouds, independent rereads, or conversation starters. They are built to reward close listening and repetition.

Because the stories repeat key world details, adults can revisit them with children without the books feeling stale.

Learning Without Pressure

Gentle learning

The learning lines use tracing, drawing, matching, observing, searching, writing, and imagining to make practice feel encouraging instead of high-pressure.

These books can support steady skill building without losing the warmth of the universe.

How to Use Learning Lines

How to use learning lines

Learning lines can be used for steady practice at home, in classrooms, or during homeschool blocks. They work best when adults keep the tone calm and encouraging.

The pages are designed to feel like story-supported practice rather than test-driven work, so they can fit into gentle routines without creating pressure.

Emotional Safety

Emotional safety

The stories avoid harsh fear, shame-based lessons, cruelty, and scary punishment framing.

The focus stays on kindness, curiosity, and repair.

Age / Use Guidance

Age and use guidance

The storybooks are written for young children and shared reading. The learning lines can also support early elementary use, depending on the child’s needs and the adult’s guidance.

Families, homeschoolers, classrooms, and libraries can choose whichever format fits the moment best: story, learning, or guided practice.
