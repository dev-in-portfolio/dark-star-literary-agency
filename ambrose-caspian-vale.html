Creative Philosophy

A story can be a doorway.

The Lulu & Ellie books are built around a simple idea: a child should be able to step into a story and feel both amazed and safe.

The worlds may include pirates, dinosaurs, moonlit circuses, space stations, camp trails, mysterious lighthouses, magical creatures, and backyard discoveries — but the heart of the story stays warm.

The adventure is not about fear. It is about noticing, helping, wondering, trying again, and finding courage in more than one form.

Why Lulu and Ellie

Two different kinds of brave.

Lulu is bold, impulsive, funny, and full of motion. She often leaps first and figures out the map second.

Ellie is careful, observant, thoughtful, and steady. She notices small clues, asks better questions, and helps the adventure slow down when it needs to.

Together, they show children that bravery can be loud or quiet, fast or careful, silly or serious.

The Worldbuilding Approach

The worldbuilding approach

The universe is built so new books can deepen the setting without confusing the reader. Recurring objects, world rules, and companion characters help young readers feel oriented from story to story.

That structure also gives the line room to support different formats: read-aloud stories, early chapter-book transitions, learning pages, companion books, and future play formats all belong to the same world.

For Parents, Teachers, and Young Readers

For parents, teachers, and young readers

The stories are made to be warm, funny, and easy to return to. They work especially well when adults want books that are imaginative without becoming overwhelming.

Young readers get a world with continuity and emotional safety. Adults get stories that support empathy, curiosity, and reading habits that can last.
